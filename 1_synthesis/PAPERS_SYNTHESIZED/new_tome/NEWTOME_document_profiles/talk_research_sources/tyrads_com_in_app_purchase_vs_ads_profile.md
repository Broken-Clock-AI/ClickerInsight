### **Abstract/Executive Summary**

This article by Daniël Boone (TyrAds) compares in-app purchases (IAPs) and in-app advertising as mobile game monetization strategies. IAPs offer personalized or faster gameplay through consumables, non-consumables, and subscriptions, generating high revenue from "whales" but risking alienation of non-paying players. In-app advertising, including banner, interstitial, rewarded, and native ads, monetizes all users, with rewarded ads being popular for in-game perks. However, ads can disrupt gameplay and typically yield lower per-user revenue than IAPs. The article concludes that the optimal choice depends on the game's genre, audience, and revenue goals, suggesting RPGs/strategy games for IAPs and casual/hyper-casual games for ads, with many successful titles adopting a hybrid model.

### **Key Sections**

1.  **Introduction:**
    *   Overview of mobile game monetization strategies: IAPs vs. Ads.

2.  **In-App Purchases (IAPs):**
    *   Definition and purpose (additional content, personalized/faster gameplay).
    *   Types: consumables (virtual currency, power-ups), non-consumables (levels, skins), subscriptions.
    *   Pros: High revenue potential (from "whales").
    *   Cons: Can alienate non-paying players, "pay-to-win" perception.

3.  **In-App Advertising (IAA):**
    *   Definition and purpose (revenue from all users).
    *   Common ad types: banner, interstitial, rewarded, native.
    *   Pros: Monetizes all players, rewarded ads popular for in-game perks.
    *   Cons: Can disrupt gameplay, generally lower revenue per user than IAPs.

4.  **Choosing the Right Strategy (IAPs vs. Ads):**
    *   Depends on game genre (RPGs/strategy for IAPs, casual/hyper-casual for ads).
    *   Depends on audience and revenue goals.
    *   Recommends hybrid models for many successful games.
