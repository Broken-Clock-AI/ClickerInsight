### **Abstract/Executive Summary**

This article delves into the philosophical underpinnings of incremental games, using *Cookie Clicker* as a primary example. It argues that *Cookie Clicker*, despite its seemingly simple mechanics, functions as a critique of capitalism and the idea of infinite growth. The author highlights the game's mechanics—such as automated clicking and upgrades that make the player's direct action less necessary—as implying an existentialist perspective, suggesting that the game could be "better played by an algorithm than a player." It references Alfie Bown's view of the game as an "accelerationist collapse of capitalism" and notes the game's meta-commentary on its own popularity.
