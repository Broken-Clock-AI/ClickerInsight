# Analysis Plan: Comprehensive Deconstruction of Idle/Incremental Games Literature

This document outlines the multi-phase methodology for analyzing and synthesizing academic literature on idle and incremental games into a comprehensive tome. This process is designed to be collaborative, with the agent (me) serving as the research engine for processing, connecting, and structuring information, and the user providing high-level direction, validating insights, and guiding the final synthesis.

---

### **Phase 1: Corpus Ingestion & High-Level Triage**

**Objective:** To create a "map" of the information landscape, understanding the scope, focus, and key arguments of each document before detailed analysis.

1.  **Content Verification:**
    *   **Action:** Quickly scan each converted Markdown file in the `markdown_conversions` directory.
    *   **Purpose:** To ensure the conversion was successful and the text is coherent and usable for further analysis.
2.  **Automated Summarization & Profiling:**
    *   **Action:** For each document, perform a high-level analysis and generate a "Document Profile."
    *   **Document Profile Components:**
        *   **Abstract/Executive Summary:** A concise summary of the paper's core thesis and findings.
        *   **Key Sections:** An outline of the document's structural elements (e.g., Introduction, Methodology, Analysis of Game X, Conclusion).
        *   **Document Type:** Classification (e.g., Academic Paper, PhD Thesis, Technical Analysis, Conference Proceeding).
        *   **Core Keywords:** A list of the most important terms and concepts discussed within the document.
3.  **Initial Report:**
    *   **Action:** Present a consolidated report of all Document Profiles to the user.
    *   **Purpose:** To provide a complete overview of the corpus, enabling the user to prioritize documents or identify key themes for initial focus.

### **Phase 2: Thematic Extraction & Atomization**

**Objective:** To deconstruct documents into fundamental, reusable concepts or "atoms" of knowledge.

1.  **Deep-Dive Analysis:**
    *   **Action:** Process the full text of each document, specifically identifying and extracting granular pieces of information.
2.  **Knowledge Atomization:**
    *   **Action:** Extract and categorize information into a structured format, using the following categories (and others as they emerge):
        *   **Definitions:** How various authors define core concepts (e.g., `Idle Game`, `Incremental Game`, `Prestige`, `Interpassivity`, `Offline Progress`).
        *   **Core Mechanics:** Atomic descriptions of game mechanics (e.g., `Exponential Growth`, `Automation`, `Ascension/Reset Loops`, `Resource Management`, `Generators`).
        *   **Player Motivations & Psychology:** Analysis of motivations for playing (e.g., `Optimization`, `Completionism`, `Relaxation`, `Compulsion Loops`).
        *   **Economic & Mathematical Models:** Notes on `Balancing Curves`, `Monetization Strategies`, `Game Theory Analyses`.
        *   **Historical Context & Canonical Examples:** References to seminal games (`Cow Clicker`, `Cookie Clicker`, `Kittens Game`, `Universal Paperclips`) and their significance.
        *   **Author Arguments & Hypotheses:** Specific claims, arguments, or conclusions presented by each author.

### **Phase 3: Cross-Corpus Synthesis & Reframing**

**Objective:** To reframe the atomized knowledge by connecting information across the entire corpus, building a holistic understanding.

1.  **Concept Mapping:**
    *   **Action:** Analyze the extracted "atoms" to identify relationships, patterns, and contradictions across all documents.
    *   **Guiding Questions:**
        *   Where do authors agree and disagree on definitions or concepts?
        *   How has the academic understanding of the genre evolved over time?
        *   What are the most frequently cited psychological principles or foundational mechanics?
2.  **Outline Generation:**
    *   **Action:** Propose a high-level structure for the final "tome," developed from the synthesized corpus, not from individual papers.
    *   **Example Structure (subject to refinement):**
        *   *Part 1: Defining the Genre*
        *   *Part 2: The Mechanical Core*
        *   *Part 3: The Player's Mind*
        *   *Part 4: The Economics of Waiting*
        *   *Part 5: A Critical Perspective - Ethics and Design*

### **Phase 4: Guided Content Generation**

**Objective:** To iteratively draft and refine the "tome" content, section by section.

1.  **Iterative Drafting:**
    *   **Action:** Generate content based on user requests for specific topics from the synthesized outline (e.g., "Write a section on 'Interpassivity', synthesizing views from Fizek and Larsson").
2.  **Evidence-Based Writing:**
    *   **Action:** Ensure all generated content is explicitly supported by source document(s), utilizing Markdown citations or footnotes for academic rigor.
3.  **Collaborative Refinement:**
    *   **Action:** Submit drafts for user review, allowing for expansion, re-evaluation of conclusions, or integration of new perspectives based on feedback.

---

This structured approach aims to be exhaustive, systematic, and to produce a robust synthesis of the available knowledge.
