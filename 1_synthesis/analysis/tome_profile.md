# Tome Profile

## Key Concepts

*   **Idle Game:** Progression without continuous player interaction.
*   **Incremental Game:** Sub-genre of idle games focused on building an internal economy and exponential growth.
*   **Interpassivity:** Deriving pleasure from delegated action.
*   **Satirical Origins:** The genre's roots in games like *Progress Quest* and *Cow Clicker*.
*   **Core Loop:** Accumulation, investment, and automation.
*   **Exponential Growth:** The "numbers getting bigger" feedback loop.
*   **Prestige/Reset (NG+):** A mechanic for extending gameplay and managing late-game plateaus.
*   **Narrative Through Abstraction:** Emergent narrative through mechanics and paradigm shifts.
*   **Player as Planner:** Shift from tactical interaction to strategic, long-term planning.
*   **Attention Economy:** Monetization of player time and attention.
*   **Calm Technology:** Designing for disengagement and player well-being.
*   **Operant Conditioning:** Use of variable reward schedules.
*   **Habit Formation:** Integration of the game into daily routines.
*   **Ludic Efficiency:** How effectively an interface supports a player's goals.
*   **Zero-Player Games:** Games that run entirely autonomously after initial setup.
*   **Minimalist Games:** Games with limited player actions and long periods of non-interaction.
*   **Clicker Games:** Games that start with intense clicking that is later automated.
