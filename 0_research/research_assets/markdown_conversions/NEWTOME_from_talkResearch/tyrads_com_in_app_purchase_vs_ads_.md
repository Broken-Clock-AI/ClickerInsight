The article "In-App Purchases vs Ads: Which One Is Better?" by Daniël Boone, published on April 8, 2025, discusses the two primary mobile game monetization strategies: in-app purchases (IAPs) and in-app advertising.[1]

IAPs allow players to buy additional content or features within a game, offering a personalized or faster gameplay experience.[1] Types of IAPs include consumables (e.g., virtual currency, power-ups), non-consumables (e.g., new levels, character skins), and subscriptions (e.g., exclusive content, premium features).[1] While IAPs can lead to high revenue, especially from "whales" (players who spend a lot), they can alienate non-paying players and create a "pay-to-win" perception.[1]

In-app advertising generates revenue by displaying ads to players, allowing developers to earn from users who may not make purchases.[1] Common ad types include banner ads, interstitial ads, rewarded ads, and native ads.[1] Ads enable monetization from all players and rewarded ads are particularly popular as they offer in-game perks without cost.[1] However, ads can disrupt gameplay and generally yield lower revenue per user compared to IAPs.[1]

The choice between IAPs and ads depends on the game's genre, audience, and revenue goals.[1] RPGs and strategy games with deep progression systems are often well-suited for IAPs, while casual, puzzle, and hyper-casual games with short play sessions are better for ads.[1] Many successful games utilize a hybrid model, combining both IAPs and ads to cater to all types of players.[1] TyrAds offers expertise to help developers choose the right monetization strategy.[1]
